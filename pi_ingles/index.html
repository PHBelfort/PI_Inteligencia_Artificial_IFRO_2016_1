ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

For centuries before Christ, philosophers have believed in the design of Artificial Intelligence, which went on to be studied there at around 50 years of our era, a time in which Alan Turing also released the article "Computing Machinery and Intelligence", which was that the machines could behave, Act and think as humans. The term Artificial Intelligence was created in 1956 at the famous meeting of Dartmouth, find this with the presence of scientists Allen Newell, Herbert Simon, Marvin Minsky, Oliver Selfridge, and John McCarthy.

Between the years 50 and 60, scientists Newell, Simon, J.C. Shaw, and envisioned a symbolic processing, which would be a system for manipulating symbols instead of a system based on numbers. From that time to the present day would have won new directions and raised many concerns because of its great technological advancement.

According to the words of Marcelo module, professor of information systems of the Methodist University of São Paulo, "there is no definition for artificial intelligence, but several [...]", the universality of Ai causes her to be branched and have various concepts according to your skills and or applications, but, basically, explains Marcelo, "[...] AI is to make computers think like humans or who are as smart as the man.

Today the man is already quite worried about the fate of IA, as the same is so revolutionized coming taking space and replacing, in some areas, the human hand in the tasks of everyday life and don't stop there, bots already have the ability to learn and to compete with humans.
